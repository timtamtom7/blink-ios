# Blink — App Store Content

## App Name
**Blink** — Video Diary

## App Icon Concept

A minimal camera lens rendered as an eye. The outer ring is a camera aperture, the inner circle is an iris, and a subtle red dot (the Blink red) sits at the center like a recording light. On a pure black background.

### Icon Layers
1. **Background**: Pure black `#000000`
2. **Outer ring**: Dark gray `#1a1a1a` — camera body
3. **Aperture blades**: 8-blade diaphragm in `#2a2a2a` — partially open
4. **Inner circle (iris)**: Dark charcoal `#141414`
5. **Center dot**: Blink red `#ff3b30` — the "blink" of the eye / recording indicator
6. **Highlight arc**: Subtle white gradient at 5% opacity — lens glass reflection

### Alternative Concept
A simple open eye, lashes suggested with clean lines, with the pupil replaced by a red circle. Very elegant, very Apple. The red pupil = the blink. Minimalist line art style.

### Full Description

**The opposite of TikTok.**

Blink is a one-tap video journal. You open it, you hit record, you talk for up to 30 seconds. It saves. That's it.

No editing. No filters. No captions. No social. No accounts. One video per day, timestamped. At the end of the year you have a 365-clip video diary of your life as it actually happened.

---

**How it works**

Open Blink. Tap the record button. You have 30 seconds — use them however you want. Talk about your day. Record a thought. Capture a feeling. When you're done, it's saved. One clip, every day.

At the end of the year, scroll through your calendar and watch 365 versions of yourself. That's your year. That's what it looked like, sounded like, felt like.

---

**Why Blink exists**

We take thousands of photos and post dozens of videos that we'll never look at again. They're optimized for others, not for us. Blink is for you. A private archive of your own life, told in 30-second fragments.

When you're 70, would you rather watch a curated highlight reel you posted to social media — or 30 seconds of an ordinary Tuesday from today?

---

**Privacy first**

Everything stays on your device. No cloud, no accounts, no algorithms. Your memories are yours alone, until you're ready to look back.

---

## Realistic Year-in-Review Concept

By December 31st, Blink shows you your year.

**The numbers:**
- 247 clips recorded (you missed 118 days — and that's OK)
- Longest clip: 29 seconds
- Shortest clip: 4 seconds
- Most common week: March, with 7 clips
- Silent month: August (you were traveling)

**What you'd see:**
A random Tuesday in February — you forgot that was a good day. Your dog's birthday. The morning you burned the toast. The night you couldn't sleep. The phone call with your dad. The trip to the coast. All of it, timestamped, real.

**The insight:**
You thought last year was hard. But watching the clips back, you smiled a lot. You just forgot.
